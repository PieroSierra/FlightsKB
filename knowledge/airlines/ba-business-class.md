## Club World Overview

**Claim type:** fact
**Applies to:** airline=BA, cabin=business
**Summary:** BA's long-haul business class product with lie-flat beds and direct aisle access on newer aircraft.

British Airways Club World is the carrier's long-haul business class product. The newest Club Suites, found on A350 and refurbished 777s, offer doors for privacy, 40 inches of legroom, and direct aisle access from every seat.

## Seat Selection Tips

**Claim type:** tactic
**Applies to:** airline=BA, cabin=business
**Summary:** Row 1 and window seats in Club Suites offer the most privacy.

For Club Suites configuration, window seats provide the most privacy as the door closes fully. Row 1 seats have extra legroom but are near the galley. On the A350, seats 11A and 11K are considered the best in the cabin.

## Lounge Access

**Claim type:** fact
**Applies to:** airline=BA, cabin=business
**Summary:** Access to BA lounges worldwide and oneworld partner lounges.

Club World passengers have access to British Airways lounges at all BA hub airports. At London Heathrow T5, this includes the Galleries Club, Galleries First (if space available), and the Concorde Room for top-tier status holders.

- **LHR T5:** Galleries Club, Galleries First
- **JFK T7:** British Airways Lounge
- **Partner lounges:** oneworld business lounges worldwide

## Upgrade Strategies

**Claim type:** tactic
**Applies to:** airline=BA, cabin=business
**Summary:** Best upgrade odds are 24 hours before departure or at check-in.

Cash upgrade offers typically appear 24-72 hours before departure via the BA app or at check-in. Avios upgrades require specific fare classes (Y, B, H on economy) and should be requested as soon as possible after booking. Executive Club Gold members receive complimentary upgrades when available.
